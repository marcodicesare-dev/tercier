## SECTION 1: CORPORATE TAX COMPARISON

### 1.1 Corporate Income Tax Rates (2025-2026)

**ZUG CANTON - BAAR MUNICIPALITY**
- Cantonal corporate income tax rate: Part of composite 11.8% total
- Municipal tax multiplier (Baar): 47.530% (lowest in Zug canton)
- Federal direct tax (on after-tax profit): 8.5% flat rate

**ZURICH CANTON - ZURICH CITY**

- Federal direct tax (on after-tax profit): 8.5% flat rate
- **Total effective corporate tax rate: 19.6%** (2025 data)
- Capital tax: 0.17% on taxable equity

**TAX SAVINGS WITH ZUG/BAAR:**
- Corporate tax savings: 7.8 percentage points (19.6% - 11.8%)
- Capital tax savings: 0.10 percentage points

### 1.2 Federal Direct Tax Detail
- Rate: 8.5% of **after-tax** profit (means ~7.8% of pre-tax profit)
- Applied uniformly across all cantons
- Non-negotiable

## SECTION 3: SOCIAL SECURITY CONTRIBUTIONS (Same Everywhere)

### 3.1 Mandatory Employee Deductions (2026)

| Contribution Type | Employee Rate | Employer Rate | Total | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AHV/IV/EO** | 5.30% | 5.30% | 10.60% | Old age, disability, survivor insurance |
| **ALV** | 1.10% | 1.10% | 2.20% | Capped at CHF 148,200/year earnings |
| **BVG/LPP** | Variable | Variable* | 50-50 split | Mandatory if income > CHF 22,680/year |
| **UVG** | Via salary | Employer | ~1.5-3% | Occupational accident insurance |

*Employer must pay at minimum 50% of total BVG contribution

### 3.2 BVG/LPP Pension Requirements

- **Mandatory entry threshold:** CHF 22,680 annual income (unchanged 2025-2026)
- **Coordination deduction (2026):** CHF 26,460
- **Insured salary:** Gross salary minus coordination deduction
- **Employer minimum contribution:** 50% of total contributions
- **Typical contribution rate:** 8-15% of insured salary (varies by fund/age)

### 3.3 UVG Accident Insurance

- **Employer obligation:** Mandatory coverage for all employees
- **Occupational accidents (BU):** Entirely employer paid (~0.1-2%)
- **Non-occupational accidents (NBU):** Typically split 50-50 or fully employee paid
- **Premium basis:** Individual employee salary/risk profile

## SECTION 6: KEY STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS

### 6.1 AG Minimum Share Capital

- **Minimum capital:** CHF 100,000 (non-negotiable)
- **Minimum paid-in at incorporation:** CHF 50,000 (20% of minimum capital)
- **Payment method:** Cash or in-kind contributions accepted
- **Process:** Money must be blocked in Swiss bank account until company registration

### 6.2 Registered Office Requirements

- **Mandatory:** Physical or virtual registered office in canton of incorporation
- **Proof required:** Lease agreement, sublease, or domicile acceptance letter
- **Founder residency:** Founder CAN live in Zurich while company is in Zug
- **Board of directors:** At least one director must be Swiss resident (Zurich resident satisfies this)
- **Substance requirement:** Company should conduct primary business in jurisdiction of incorporation
  - *Exception:* Permanent establishment created if business conducted primarily elsewhere

### 6.3 Commercial Register

- Company must register with Zug (or Zurich) Commercial Register
- Public registration document
- Requires certified Articles of Association/bylaws
- One-time registration cost (~CHF 500-1,500)

### 9.1 Clear Winner: Zug/Baar for Most Scenarios

**Annual Tax/Social Cost Difference (CHF 72,000 salary):**
- **Employee gets home:** +CHF 7,200-10,080/year
- **Monthly advantage:** +CHF 600-840
- **Corporate tax savings (CHF 100K profit):** +CHF 7,900/year
- **Capital tax savings:** -CHF 100/year

**Five-year impact (employee + business):**
- Personal income tax savings: CHF 36,000-50,000
- Corporate tax savings (assuming CHF 100K profit): CHF 39,500
- **Total five-year savings: CHF 75,500-89,500**

### 9.2 Zug Advantages Summary

1. **Lower personal income tax** - 8-10% effective vs 18-20% in Zurich
2. **Lower corporate tax** - 11.8% vs 19.6% (7.8 percentage point difference)
3. **Lower capital tax** - 0.07% vs 0.17%
4. **Founder can live in Zurich** - Only need registered office in Zug
5. **Crypto/startup ecosystem** - 719 blockchain companies, CV Labs accelerator
6. **New R&D incentives** - CHF 150M/year program (2026-2028)
7. **Minimal bureaucratic friction** - Same registration process as other cantons

### 9.3 When Zurich City Makes Sense

- Registered office/physical presence must be in Zurich for client reasons
- Business primarily conducted in Zurich (risk of permanent establishment)
- Company wants Zurich brand prestige over tax savings
- Not applicable for pure software/remote companies
