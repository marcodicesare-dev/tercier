Methodology: What This Is (and Isn't)

What We Did

  • Loaded 718 real Swiss hotel dossiers with website-derived enrichment data (star rating, rooms, ADR, positioning, booking infrastructure, audience signals)
  • Applied ICP filter: 4+ stars, ADR >= CHF 250, enriched website data. Result: 272 hotels
  • Simulated 2 buyer roles per hotel: GM/Owner and Commercial/Marketing Director
  • 3 independent replications per hotel-role to measure consistency (1,632 total interviews)
  • Each interview covers: problem salience (7 dimensions), Gabor-Granger pricing (6 price points), purchase justifiers, deal killers, proof requirements
  • Adversarial critic pass to challenge optimistic assumptions
  • Judge pass (GPT-5) for final calibration with strict confidence criteria
  • Conservative merge of GM + Commercial perspectives per hotel

What This Is NOT

  • Not real survey data. These are synthetic respondents grounded in real hotel characteristics
  • Cannot capture actual internal politics, personal relationships, or hidden budget constraints
  • Hotels with limited website data have less grounded responses
  • Replication consistency was high (541/544 groups fully distinct), but some variance is expected

Data Quality Checks

  • 541/544 hotel-role groups had fully distinct replications (no copy-paste)
  • 272/272 hotels have segment + revenue data from enriched master CSV
  • 0 slug mismatches across all pipeline phases
  • Deal killers canonicalized into 9 clean categories
  • Judge confidence: 21% high, 63% medium, 17% low

Treat these results as hypothesis-grade intelligence for outreach prioritization. Validate the top 10-20 targets with real discovery calls before committing to any pricing or go-to-market strategy.

What Makes Tercier a No-Brainer

At CHF 3,000/month

3 Required Outcomes (most cited):

  1. Measurable uplift in direct bookings/revenue within 90 days
  2. Competitive intelligence that outperforms what they get from STR/OTA reports
  3. Time savings equivalent to at least 1 FTE's weekly commercial admin

3 Required Capabilities:

  1. Seamless PMS/CRM integration (no manual data entry)
  2. Actionable weekly briefs (not just dashboards -- specific recommendations)
  3. Multilingual content generation that matches the hotel's brand voice

The One-Slide Number:

"We'll prove a 5-10% direct booking uplift in 90 days, or you walk."

Max implementation: 14-21 days | Min proof: 3 months | ROI believers: GM + Revenue/Commercial Director

At CHF 5,000/month

Extra Value Required:

  • Full revenue attribution tied to platform actions
  • Proven ROI of 5-10x the subscription cost
  • Performance-based pricing component (shared risk)
  • Dedicated account manager / white-glove support

Non-Negotiables at CHF 5k:

  • GDPR compliance + Swiss/EU data hosting
  • Monthly opt-out (no annual lock-in at this price)
  • Proven Swiss hotel references (same category)

Remaining Blockers:

  • Owner/board approval required for this spend level
  • Competing priorities (renovation, PMS migration, staff)
  • Seasonal cash flow constraints

The gap between CHF 3k and CHF 5k is not just price sensitivity -- it's a fundamentally different buyer conversation. At CHF 3k, you're selling operational efficiency to a commercial director. At CHF 5k, you're selling revenue attribution to an owner/board, and they need hard proof.

Recommendations and Next Steps

Pricing Strategy

  1. Launch at CHF 1,500-2,000/month. 89% of the market lands in CHF 1k-2k. Don't fight the data.
  2. Expansion path to CHF 3k after 90-day proof period with demonstrated ROI. Only 10% of the market accepts CHF 2k-3k upfront.
  3. CHF 5k+ is an enterprise play for large properties (150+ rooms, CHF 20M+ revenue) only after multiple Swiss references exist.
  4. Performance component: Consider 70% base + 30% performance at higher tiers to reduce perceived risk.

Go-to-Market Priority

  1. Validate top 10 with real calls. These are all 5-star urban/resort hotels with 80+ rooms. If 3-4 convert to pilots, the research is validated.
  2. Build PMS integration first. 79% say it's the deal killer. Without it, nothing else matters.
  3. Secure 2-3 Swiss references. Second-highest proof requirement. Even paid pilots count.
  4. GDPR/Swiss hosting is table stakes. Not a differentiator -- a prerequisite.

Sales Playbook Implications

  • Lead with "clearer weekly decisions" -- the #1 justifier at 99% mention rate
  • Sell to Commercial Director first (45% of budget comes from commercial/revenue). GM signs off but doesn't champion.
  • 60-day pilot is the foot in the door. Ranks #3 in proof requirements. Reduces commitment anxiety.
  • Replacement anchor: "This replaces the 10 hours/week your team spends on manual competitive monitoring + review analysis"
  • Expect 12+ month sales cycles for 67% of the market. Build pipeline accordingly.

What NOT to Do

  • Don't lead with AI discovery/visibility -- it's where the market will be in 18 months, not where it is today. Build it as a moat, not a wedge.
  • Don't price above CHF 2k for first contract without a reference in their exact segment
  • Don't skip the adversarial findings: the 67% delay rate is real
  • Don't treat this data as ground truth -- it's hypothesis-grade. Validate with real conversations.
