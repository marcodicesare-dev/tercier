# What Hotels Actually Buy — The Agency Replacement Thesis

> Distilled from 272-hotel synthetic survey, Corsaro/Guffanti call transcripts, 100+ industry sources
> **Updated April 2026:** Reframed from "monthly deliverables" to platform capabilities. The research findings are unchanged — the product framing now reflects Lumina as an autonomous AI platform, not a human-produced service.

## The Product Is a Platform, Not a Service

Hotels don't buy platforms for the sake of having a login. They buy outcomes. Lumina replaces the fragmented agency stack with an always-on AI commercial intelligence platform. The hotel's marketing person (1 person running €15-70M hotel's entire marketing) uses Lumina as their AI-powered commercial co-pilot.

## The 5 Core Platform Capabilities

These are live views and on-demand tools in the platform — NOT monthly reports delivered by Lumina employees.

### 1. "What Changed" — Live Competitive Intelligence Feed
Demand shifts, events approaching, competitor moves, source market signals, new review patterns. Updated continuously as data flows in. The hotel sees what changed TODAY, not in a monthly email.

### 2. "Who Matters Now" — Guest Persona Dashboard
Which guest segments to target based on real signals. Dynamic — updates as new reviews, booking data, and seasonal patterns emerge. Not a static quarterly persona deck.

### 3. "Where You're Losing" — Competitive Gap Analysis
Your hotel through the eyes of a specific guest segment, side by side with the competitor winning that guest. Always current. The hotel can drill into any competitor, any segment, any time.

### 4. "What to Do About It" — AI Decision Engine
5 ranked priorities by ROI. Not 50 suggestions. The platform's Decision Engine (Layer 6) synthesizes all intelligence and surfaces: "This week: push the wellness narrative — your reviews are 4.8/5 on spa but your website buries it, and competitor X just dropped their spa prices."

### 5. "Here It Is, Done" — On-Demand Content Generation
Landing pages, campaign inputs, newsletter copy, OTA descriptions, FAQ blocks. Property-specific. Persona-targeted. Multilingual. Generated by the AI on demand when the hotel marketing person needs it. They click "generate" → review → publish. No Lumina human in the loop.

## The AI Discovery Hook

- **HOOK:** "84% of hotels are invisible to AI. Let me show you how ChatGPT sees your property."
- **SUBSTANCE:** "The reason you're invisible is content infrastructure. Lumina fixes that continuously."
- **ROI:** "Shifting 5% of bookings from OTA to direct saves EUR 112K/year. This costs EUR 12-60K/year."

AI discovery creates the meeting. The platform capabilities close the deal.

## Corsaro's Key Insight: Opacity as Moat

> "The more hermetic we become about what's behind it, while ensuring constant investment in the final intelligence... we will always win."

Don't show the 7-layer machinery. Sell it as an AI-powered commercial co-pilot. The how doesn't matter. The output matters.

## Corsaro's "Virtual Revenue Manager"

> "Go to the buyer and say: I'm pairing you with a virtual revenue manager you can talk to. You don't have a guided UX — just ask me."

The interface should be conversational, not a dashboard with 7 tabs. The complexity is invisible. The platform reads, decides, and generates. The hotel directs, reviews, and publishes.
