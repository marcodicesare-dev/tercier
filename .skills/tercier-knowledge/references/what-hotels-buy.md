# What Hotels Actually Buy — The Agency Replacement Thesis

> Distilled from 272-hotel synthetic survey, Corsaro/Guffanti call transcripts, 100+ industry sources

## The Product Is a Deliverable, Not a Login

Hotels don't buy platforms. They buy outcomes. The product that makes money is the **agency replacement** — a virtual commercial team member.

## The 5 Monthly Deliverables

### 1. "What Changed"
Demand shifts, events approaching, competitor moves, source market signals, new review patterns. A 2-page brief: what's different this month for YOUR property.

### 2. "Who Matters Now"
Which guest segments to target this month based on real signals. Not assumptions — data.

### 3. "Where You're Losing"
Your hotel through the eyes of a specific guest segment, side by side with the competitor winning that guest.

### 4. "What to Do About It"
5 ranked priorities. Not 50 suggestions. This week: fix the suite descriptions. Next week: publish the event page.

### 5. "Here It Is, Done"
Landing pages, campaign inputs, newsletter copy, OTA descriptions, FAQ blocks. Property-specific. Persona-targeted. Multilingual. Ready to review and publish.

## The AI Discovery Hook

- **HOOK:** "84% of hotels are invisible to AI. Let me show you how ChatGPT sees your property."
- **SUBSTANCE:** "The reason you're invisible is content infrastructure. Tercier fixes that continuously."
- **ROI:** "Shifting 5% of bookings from OTA to direct saves EUR 112K/year. This costs EUR 30-60K/year."

AI discovery creates the meeting. The monthly deliverables close the deal.

## Corsaro's Key Insight: Opacity as Moat

> "The more hermetic we become about what's behind it, while ensuring constant investment in the final intelligence... we will always win."

Don't show the 7-layer machinery. Sell it as a virtual commercial team member. The how doesn't matter. The output matters.

## Corsaro's "Virtual Revenue Manager"

> "Go to the buyer and say: I'm pairing you with a virtual revenue manager you can talk to. You don't have a guided UX — just ask me."

The interface should be conversational, not a dashboard with 7 tabs. The complexity is invisible.
