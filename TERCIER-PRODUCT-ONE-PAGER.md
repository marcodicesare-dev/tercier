## Role-Play Iteration Log

### Round 1: Sofia, Marketing Coordinator, Hotel Principe di Savoia (Milan). 4 years experience. Reports to Regional Marketing Director.

**Her actual Tuesday:**
She opens Canva to resize a social post the agency sent three weeks late. She answers an email from the regional team asking for "the competitor overview for next quarter" that nobody has built because there is no system that does it. She Googles "events Milan April 2026" to try to figure out what to push in the newsletter that goes out Friday. She writes the newsletter in English, sends it to an external translator for Italian and German, gets it back Thursday, loads it into Mailchimp, and blasts it to the full list because nobody has segmented the database by guest type. Open rate: 18%. Booking revenue from email this quarter: almost zero.

She knows the hotel has Japanese honeymooners, American luxury families, German business travelers, and Italian weekend couples. She speaks English and Italian. She has never written a word targeting the Japanese or German segments because she cannot. She has never audited the hotel website through the eyes of a German business traveler because she does not know what a German business traveler expects from a luxury hotel website. She has no competitive intelligence beyond what she picks up from Instagram and the occasional industry event.

**What would make Sofia's eyes light up:**
She logs into Tercier. The platform already knows Hotel Principe di Savoia. Agents have already ingested 2,100 reviews across four platforms, identified five guest archetypes (German Business Traveler, American Luxury Family, Japanese Honeymooner, Italian Weekend Couple, Middle Eastern Luxury Shopper), mapped the competitive set (Park Hyatt, Bulgari, Mandarin Oriental, Four Seasons), and detected that Salone del Mobile is 6 weeks out and will spike Italian design-crowd demand.

She clicks "German Business Traveler." She sees exactly what this archetype cares about, pulled from hundreds of real German-language reviews: fast check-in, quiet rooms, meeting facilities, restaurant quality for client dinners, airport transfer reliability. She sees how the hotel scores vs. Park Hyatt on each dimension, through the German traveler's eyes. She clicks "generate newsletter for German Business Traveler, in German, about the Salone del Mobile corporate hospitality package." Tercier produces it. She reviews it, adjusts two sentences, schedules it. The newsletter goes out in German, targeting the German business segment, about something happening in six weeks that matters to them.

She did in 30 minutes what used to take two weeks, three external vendors, and a translator. And the old version was a generic English blast to the whole list.

**Would Sofia's hotel pay EUR 2,000/month for this?** Sofia does not control the budget. Her Regional Marketing Director does. But Sofia would walk into that Director's office and say: "This thing just let me produce targeted multilingual content for five guest segments in a morning. I've never been able to do that."

**Would the Regional Marketing Director pay?** The Director manages 8 properties across Italy. Each property has a Sofia. The Director currently pays an agency EUR 5,000-8,000/month per property for generic content that arrives late and reads the same for every hotel. If Tercier gives each property's Sofia the ability to produce persona-targeted, multilingual content that is actually specific to that property's guest mix, comp-set, and market signals, the Director replaces or significantly reduces the agency retainer. EUR 2,000-3,000 per property is an easy yes. At 8 properties, that is EUR 16,000-24,000/month for the cluster, replacing EUR 40,000-64,000/month in agency fees.

### Round 2: Hans, General Manager, Independent 5-star hotel in Lucerne. 180 rooms. EUR 22M revenue. No chain support.

**His actual pain:**
Hans has one marketing person (part-time, also handles events). His hotel gets guests from Switzerland, Germany, USA, China, and the Gulf states. Five languages, five completely different travel motivations. His marketing person posts on Instagram twice a week and updates the website seasonally. Hans has no idea how his hotel appears when someone asks ChatGPT "best luxury hotel in Lucerne." He has no idea what his competitors are doing with their digital presence beyond occasionally checking their websites. He has no idea what his Chinese guests specifically value vs. his German guests, beyond anecdotal conversation at breakfast.

He pays TrustYou EUR 180/month for review scores. He pays a local agency EUR 3,000/month for generic social media management. He reads his own reviews on Booking.com when he has time, maybe 10-15 per week out of 200+ that come in monthly.

**What would make Hans write a check immediately:**
Hans opens Tercier. He sees that the platform has read every review his hotel has received in the past two years, across all platforms, in every language. The system has identified that his Chinese guests (14% of bookings) consistently mention "lake view room" and "Swiss chocolate experience" as reasons they chose his hotel over competitors, but they also consistently complain about breakfast options and Mandarin-speaking staff. His German guests (28% of bookings) care about parking, spa quality, and value for money relative to comparable properties in Interlaken. His American guests (11%) love the location and the concierge but find the room technology outdated.

He has never seen this level of structured intelligence about his own guests. His TrustYou dashboard gives him a 4.3 score and "staff: positive." Tercier tells him exactly which guest segment is underserved and what to fix.

Then he clicks "evaluate my website through the eyes of a Chinese honeymooner" and sees a side-by-side comparison with Bürgenstock Resort: his website has no Chinese-language content, no mention of the experiences Chinese honeymooners specifically search for, and his room photography does not highlight the lake view that Chinese reviews consistently cite as the deciding factor. Bürgenstock has all of this.

Then he asks Tercier to generate a Chinese-language landing page targeting honeymooners, a German newsletter about the new spa renovation, and an English blog post about the autumn events calendar. All three, targeted to the right persona, in the right language, ready to review, in 20 minutes.

**Would Hans pay EUR 1,500/month?** Hans currently spends EUR 3,000/month on an agency that gives him generic social posts. Tercier replaces the agency AND gives him market intelligence, competitive analysis, persona-driven evaluation, and multilingual content production he has never had access to. EUR 1,500/month is half what he pays for worse output. It is a replacement, not an addition.

**Would Hans pay EUR 3,000/month?** If he cancels the agency, yes. He is spending the same amount and getting 10x the output. The content is actually about his hotel, his guests, his market. The agency content could be about any hotel in Switzerland.

### Round 5: Investor due diligence. Hard numbers.

**Revenue leakage the product addresses:**
- OTA commissions on a EUR 25M hotel at 50% OTA mix and 18% average commission: EUR 2.25M/year going to Booking.com and Expedia.
- Shifting 5% of OTA bookings to direct saves EUR 112K/year. Tercier costs EUR 24-60K/year. ROI is 2-5x on commission savings alone, before any uplift from better-targeted marketing.

**Content production economics:**
- A hotel newsletter with proper segmentation and personalization generates $1,249 average booking value per conversion (Revinate 2025 benchmark). Targeted segments under 5,000 contacts convert at 8x the rate of blast emails.
- A hotel that sends one generic English newsletter per month to its entire list vs. a hotel that sends four persona-targeted newsletters per month in four languages: the second hotel is producing 16x the relevant content. Tercier makes the second scenario possible without adding headcount.

**Time savings:**
- 80% of hotels spend up to two full workdays per week pulling reports together (State of Distribution Report 2025). Tercier's intelligence agents automate the market analysis, competitive monitoring, and review synthesis that constitutes most of that reporting.
- Manual review analysis of 1,700 reviews across four platforms in six languages: 40+ hours for a human analyst who speaks all six languages (which no single person does). Tercier processes it in minutes.

## The One-Pager

### What Tercier does, in one sentence

Tercier's AI agents turn a single hotel marketing person into a multilingual commercial team by reading every guest review, mapping the competitive set, building data-driven personas, and producing targeted content for each guest segment in their native language.

### The problem, in plain language

A premium hotel generating EUR 15-70M in revenue is a medium-sized business. It has guests arriving from 5-8 countries, each traveling for different reasons, each expecting content and communication in their own language and cultural frame. A hotel in Dubai has Indian families, British couples, German groups, Saudi shoppers, American tourists, and Japanese honeymooners. Each of these segments responds to different messages, compares the hotel to different competitors, and books through different channels.

The marketing department for this hotel is typically one person. Sometimes two. The person has 3-5 years of experience, speaks two languages, and handles everything from social media to the TV welcome screen. The chain's corporate marketing team produces brand guidelines and buys media centrally, but they do not produce property-specific content for each hotel's unique guest mix and local market. They cannot. There are hundreds of hotels in the chain and each one is different.

The result: every hotel communicates generically. One language, one message, one newsletter blast to the entire list. The German business traveler and the Japanese honeymooner receive the same email. The marketing person has no structured intelligence about what each guest segment actually values, what competitors do better, what demand signals are approaching, or how the hotel appears in AI-powered travel search. The hotel leaves direct bookings on the table because it cannot match the sophistication of its own guest base.

### What the hotel gets from Tercier

Tercier is a platform with specialized AI agents. The hotel's marketing person opens it and works with it daily. The agents have already done the heavy lifting:

**Understanding the market.** Agents collect incoming market data for the hotel's specific city, identify the competitive set at the local level, track events that drive demand, and map source markets by country, travel motivation, and seasonal pattern. The marketing person sees a live commercial context specific to their property.

**Understanding the guests.** Agents ingest 1,500-2,000+ reviews across Booking, Google, TripAdvisor, and Expedia, in every language. They extract structured intelligence: what each nationality values, what disappoints them, what drives their hotel choice, how sentiment compares to each competitor on specific dimensions. From this data, agents build 3-7 guest archetypes per hotel. Each archetype is grounded in what real guests actually wrote, not in marketing assumptions.

**Seeing through the guest's eyes.** The marketing person selects a persona and asks Tercier to evaluate anything through that persona's lens. The hotel website, a competitor's website, a room description, a package offer. Tercier returns a structured evaluation: where the hotel wins, where it loses, what to fix, scored against what that specific guest type cares about. This is the work of a senior brand strategist. Tercier does it in minutes, backed by hundreds of real reviews instead of gut feeling.

**Producing content that fits.** The marketing person selects a persona, a language, a channel, and a brief. Tercier generates the content natively in that language, with cultural context, in the hotel's brand voice. A German newsletter about the spa renovation. An Arabic Instagram caption for Ramadan. A Japanese landing page for cherry blossom season packages. An English email about a corporate event. All persona-driven, all property-specific, all ready to review and publish.

**Planning the work.** Agents generate a content calendar per persona with priorities mapped to demand signals and competitive gaps. The marketing person follows a structured plan instead of guessing what to post this week.

### The concrete output, in numbers

A hotel using Tercier can realistically produce:
- 20-40 pieces of persona-targeted, multilingual content per month (vs. 4-8 generic, single-language pieces without Tercier)
- Competitive intelligence updated continuously across 4-6 local competitors (vs. occasional manual website checks)
- Guest sentiment analysis across 1,500+ reviews in all languages (vs. reading 10-15 reviews per week on Booking.com)
- 3-7 validated guest personas with real review data (vs. zero structured personas at most properties)
- Event-triggered content matched to demand windows (vs. reacting late or missing the window entirely)

### Who buys it

The buyer is the Commercial Director or Director of Sales & Marketing at a premium or luxury hotel with EUR 15-70M revenue, ADR above EUR 250, and guests from multiple source markets. The user is the marketing person at the property level.

For hotel groups, the buyer is the Regional or Cluster Marketing Director who manages 5-15 properties and currently outsources to agencies that produce generic content at EUR 3,000-8,000 per property per month.

### Why the buyer pays without hesitation

**Replacement math.** The hotel currently pays EUR 3,000-8,000/month for agency content that is generic, late, and monolingual. Tercier produces better, faster, persona-targeted, multilingual output for EUR 1,000-5,000/month. It is a cost reduction with a quality upgrade.

**Capability math.** Hiring a senior marketer, a market analyst, and a multilingual content lead costs EUR 180-300K/year fully loaded. Tercier provides equivalent capability for EUR 24-60K/year.

**Revenue math.** One newsletter sent to a properly segmented audience generates $1,249 average booking value per conversion (Revinate 2025). Targeted segments convert at 8x the rate of blast emails. A hotel that goes from one generic monthly blast to four persona-targeted multilingual campaigns per month is not marginally better. It is operating in a different category.

**Commission math.** A EUR 25M hotel paying 18% OTA commission on 50% of bookings sends EUR 2.25M/year to Booking.com. Shifting 5% of OTA bookings to direct through better content and direct-booking positioning saves EUR 112K/year. Tercier costs EUR 24-60K/year. The commission savings alone return 2-5x the investment.
