2. MONTHLY P&L SHEET - CRITICAL ERROR FOUND ✗

   *** ERROR #1: ROW 8 (CHAIN PAYING HOTELS) FORMULA IS BROKEN ***
   
   Location: Monthly P&L, Row 8, Columns E onwards
   
   What it should do:
   - Show chain hotels that have completed their 3-month free trial
   - M1-M3: All zeros (chains still in trial)
   - M4+: Active chain count minus those still in trial
   
   What it actually does:
   - B8-D8: Hardcoded to 0 ✓ (correct)
   - E8: =B5 ✗ (references M1 active instead of M4 active)
   - F8: =C5 ✗ (references M2 active instead of M5 active)
   - G8: =D5 ✗ (references M3 active instead of M6 active)
   - H8: =E5 ✗ (references M4 active instead of M7 active)
   
   This creates a 1-4 month lag and incorrect reference.
   
   IMPACT EXAMPLE:
   Month 4 (Kempinski's first paid month after 3-month trial):
   - Current calculation: 3 hotels @ 1000 EUR = EUR 3,000
   - Correct calculation: 82 hotels @ 1000 EUR = EUR 82,000
   - Understatement: -27x (97.5% error)
   
   Revenue cascade:
   - M4 revenue: EUR 3,000 actual vs EUR 82,000 expected
   - M5 revenue: EUR 3,000 actual vs EUR 82,000 expected
   - M7 revenue: EUR 5,500 actual vs ~120,000 expected
   - M10 revenue: EUR 23,500 actual vs ~225,000 expected
   
   *** ERROR #2: INCONSISTENT ROLLOUT LOGIC ***
   
   Location: Rows 5 & 8
   
   Problem:
   - Row 5 (Chain active) applies rollout percentages from Assumptions E24:H24
     Example: Kempinski at M6 = 82 * 0.15 = 12.3 active
   - Row 8 (Chain paying) doesn't apply rollout logic
   - This creates inconsistency: "active" has rollout, "paying" doesn't
   
   The correct approach would be:
   - Row 5: Apply rollout %
   - Row 8: Apply same rollout % AND subtract those in trial
   
   Currently they're misaligned.
