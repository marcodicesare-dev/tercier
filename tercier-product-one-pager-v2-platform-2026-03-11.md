Purpose:
- Define Tercier as the product it actually is
- Correct the mistake of describing it like a recurring report
- Keep the wedge, but express it as a platform with orchestrated agents that do real work

## What Tercier is

Tercier is a vertical AI platform for premium and luxury hotel commercial teams.

It is not a dashboard and it is not just a monthly report.

It is a system of specialized agents that:
- collect and structure market and hotel data
- build property-specific guest personas
- read the hotel and its competitors through those personas
- turn that intelligence into priorities, calendars, and content
- execute part of the work directly through a content engine

The point of the product is not to "show analysis." The point is to give each hotel the operating capacity of a stronger local commercial and content team.

## The job Tercier is hired to do

When a hotel needs to understand its local demand, incoming markets, guest archetypes, and competitive position, then act on that understanding fast across content and positioning, Tercier does the analysis and the execution work that the local team cannot realistically do alone.

## Why this exists

The transcript is clear:
- each hotel is its own ecosystem
- incoming markets differ property by property
- source markets, languages, motivations, and comp sets differ city by city
- chain HQ cannot deeply serve every property
- the local marketer is usually too junior and too overloaded

So the problem is not "lack of marketing tools."

The problem is:
- no property-level commercial intelligence layer
- no scalable way to turn that intelligence into action
- no internal capacity to produce persona-specific multilingual content at the speed required

## The core product

Tercier is an orchestrated platform made of six working layers.

### 1. Market and location intelligence layer

Tercier identifies the hotel's market context:
- source markets
- local demand drivers
- relevant events
- location-specific dynamics
- property-specific competitive set

This gives the platform the commercial context that generic AI tools do not have.

### 2. Voice-of-customer and review intelligence layer

Tercier ingests review data and related signals across languages, then structures them into usable hotel intelligence:
- recurring themes
- perceived strengths and weaknesses
- travel motivations
- guest expectations
- differences between what the hotel thinks it is and what guests actually value

### 3. Persona construction layer

Tercier uses market data plus review intelligence to build hotel-specific personas.

These are not generic "business traveler" templates. They are property-specific archetypes grounded in:
- who actually comes to this hotel
- why they travel
- what they care about
- what they praise or complain about
- what content and offers are likely to resonate

### 4. Competitive reading layer

Tercier can evaluate the hotel and its competitors through the lens of a specific persona.

This is a core part of the product.

It means the team can ask:
- for this persona, which hotel wins and why?
- where is our positioning weak?
- which competitor communicates better to this segment?
- what are we not saying that matters to this audience?

### 5. Strategy and prioritization layer

Tercier does not stop at analysis.

It produces:
- action priorities
- per-persona strategic direction
- content and campaign opportunities
- event-driven recommendations
- channel-specific suggestions
- a working calendar linked to the hotel's real market context

This is the "orchestrator" part from the call, not just analytics.

### 6. Content execution layer

Tercier includes a content engine.

It can draft:
- newsletters
- social posts
- landing page or website copy
- room and suite descriptions
- OTA copy
- event-driven content
- other marketing assets

And it can do this:
- by persona
- by language
- by channel
- by strategic objective

This is why the product is not just intelligence software. It also executes a meaningful part of the work.

## How the platform is used

The user does not just receive a PDF.

Inside Tercier, the hotel team can:
- select the property and comp set
- inspect the incoming markets and personas
- compare itself to competitors through a chosen persona
- see the action priorities and strategic calendar
- ask Tercier to generate or adapt content for a chosen audience, language, and channel
- use the platform as both intelligence system and production engine

The user experience should feel like a hotel-specific commercial agent, not like a static reporting tool.

## What the customer gets from the platform

The platform produces outputs, but the outputs are consequences of the system.

The hotel gets:
- property-specific market intelligence
- review intelligence across languages
- dynamic guest personas
- persona-based competitive analysis
- action priorities and strategy suggestions
- a working content calendar
- multilingual drafted content

So the product is a platform, and the value is both:
- ongoing access to the intelligence system
- the work product the system continuously generates

## The first wedge

The first wedge is still narrow.

It should be:
- property-level commercial intelligence plus content execution
- for premium/luxury hotels with lean teams and direct-booking pressure

That means the platform should first be sold around:
- understanding who matters for this property
- understanding how the property is positioned versus its comp set
- turning that into better and faster communication

Not around:
- generic AI writing
- generic marketing automation
- a vague "hospitality copilot"

## Why this is a platform business and not a service disguised as software

Tercier becomes a platform because the same system can be reused across hotels with property-specific inputs:
- market ingestion
- review ingestion
- persona generation
- competitor reading
- strategy generation
- content generation

The human team does not manually recreate the work each time from scratch.

Instead, the platform runs a repeatable set of agents on top of each hotel's context and data.

That is the product.

## Why a hotel buys

The hotel buys because Tercier gives it capabilities it does not have today:
- a way to interpret its real local market
- a way to model who its guests actually are
- a way to understand positioning through those personas
- a way to move from analysis to action without waiting on HQ or agencies
- a way to produce better multilingual content without adding headcount

The value is not "AI."

The value is compressed commercial capability.

## What Tercier is not

Tercier is not:
- a reputation tool only
- a content tool only
- a dashboard only
- a generic horizontal AI platform

Tercier is:
- a hotel-specific commercial intelligence and execution platform powered by specialized agents
