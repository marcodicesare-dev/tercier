Purpose:

- sharpen the product around the most buyable workflow
- integrate the AI discovery / AI visibility angle into the product itself, not as a side note

## One sentence

Tercier helps premium hotels win more direct bookings and higher-value guests by deciding what each property should say, promote, and publish each month from live market, review, competitor, and AI-discovery signals.

## What Tercier is

Tercier is a property-level commercial execution platform for premium and luxury hotels.

Tercier reads the market around a property, the guests already coming, the competitors winning nearby, and the way the property appears across direct channels and AI answers. It then decides what matters now and drafts the pages, campaigns, and content the team needs to ship.

Tercier gives each property a stronger commercial team than it can afford to hire locally.

## Why this exists

Each hotel is its own market.

A premium hotel in Milan, Dubai, or the Amalfi Coast has different:
- source markets
- traveler intents
- event calendars
- booking windows
- languages
- competitor sets
- guest expectations

Most hotel groups still cannot give each property a serious local commercial function. Local teams are thin. HQ support is generic. Agencies are slow. Review tools, reputation tools, revenue tools, and content workflows sit in separate places.

That creates:
- missed demand windows
- generic property messaging
- slow reaction to local events and traveler shifts
- weak direct-channel execution
- poor visibility in AI-mediated travel discovery

## Why now

The distribution environment changed.

Cloudbeds found that in AI hotel recommendations, `55.3%` of cited sources came from OTAs and `72.4%` of recommended hotels were branded or large-group properties. BCG and NYU reported on March 2, 2026 that `37%` of travelers already use AI large language models embedded in travel sites to plan and book trips. Lighthouse and The Hotels Network are already positioning ChatGPT-native hotel booking flows as a new direct channel.

This means the hotel's website is no longer the only first impression. The hotel's story is increasingly assembled inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI-enabled travel surfaces.

If the property's information is generic, stale, weakly structured, or missing intent-specific proof, the AI layer fills the gaps with OTAs, scraped summaries, and stronger brands.

Tercier exists to stop that drift.

## The job Tercier is hired to do

When a premium hotel needs to win the right guests at the right rate for this property, but the local team is too thin to continuously read the market, rewrite the message, and ship the assets, Tercier turns fragmented signals into property-specific commercial action.

That job has four parts:
- understand which guest segments and demand moments matter now
- understand how the property wins or loses for those segments
- decide what the hotel should change, push, or publish next
- generate the assets needed to act before the month is lost

## The wedge

The first wedge is property-specific direct-demand execution.

That means Tercier is first sold around one painful recurring need:
- deciding what the property should push now
- fixing weak direct-channel and discovery-layer messaging
- generating ready-to-ship content for the team to act fast

This wedge is stronger than selling personas alone, competitor analysis alone, or generic AI content generation.

It sits closer to revenue, existing budgets, and visible daily work.

## The core platform

Tercier works as an orchestrated system of seven layers.

### 1. Market and location intelligence

Tercier identifies the property's commercial context:
- source markets
- local demand drivers
- seasonality and booking windows
- major events and periods of compression
- property-specific comp set

This gives Tercier the local commercial reality that generic tools miss.

### 2. Voice-of-customer intelligence

Tercier ingests review and guest-feedback signals across languages and extracts:
- recurring strengths and weaknesses
- traveler motivations
- unmet expectations
- phrases guests actually use
- proof points that matter by segment

This turns reviews into commercial input, not just sentiment reporting.

### 3. Persona and intent modeling

Tercier builds property-specific demand models around real guest types and traveler intents.

These are not broad templates like "business traveler" or "family segment." Tercier models:
- who actually chooses this property
- why they choose it
- what anxieties they have before booking
- which claims they trust
- which competitor messages outperform the property for that intent

### 4. Competitive reading

Tercier reads the property and the comp set through the lens of a chosen guest type or traveler intent.

That lets the team see:
- where the property is under-explaining value
- where a competitor is clearer or more desirable
- which proof points are missing
- which pages, messages, and offers are weak for a specific segment

### 5. AI discovery and visibility intelligence

Tercier monitors how the property is represented in AI-mediated discovery and where the narrative breaks.

This layer looks for gaps such as:
- OTA dominance over the hotel's own narrative
- missing answers to common traveler questions
- stale or generic property descriptions
- weak machine-readable property facts
- missing segment-specific or event-specific content
- missing proof that helps AI systems trust a claim

This layer protects the property's direct channel and brand narrative when travelers ask AI where to stay.

### 6. Decision and prioritization engine

Tercier combines market, review, persona, comp set, and AI-discovery signals into a ranked execution queue.

Tercier decides:
- which guest segments matter now
- which demand moments deserve immediate action
- which messages or pages are underperforming
- which new pages or assets should exist
- which updates are most likely to improve direct demand and visibility

### 7. Content and asset engine

Tercier drafts the assets the team needs to ship.

This includes:
- landing pages and direct-booking pages
- event and seasonal pages
- suite, room, and amenity copy
- newsletters and campaign inputs
- OTA and directory copy
- FAQ blocks and answer assets for traveler questions
- multilingual variations by segment and channel

These assets are not generic copy.

Tercier shapes them for both human conversion and AI retrieval. The content engine uses the principles that show up repeatedly in the GEO / AEO research corpus:
- clear entity naming and property facts
- self-contained answer blocks under descriptive headings
- review-grounded proof points
- extractable formats such as bullets, tables, and FAQs
- structured data hooks and machine-readable facts
- freshness markers for seasonal and event-driven content
- consistency across direct pages, listings, and other surfaces

## What Tercier changes in AI discovery

Tercier folds AI visibility into the commercial workflow.

For a premium hotel, that means:
- the official property site becomes a stronger source of truth for AI systems
- key traveler intents get dedicated, answerable, segment-specific pages
- weak or vague property claims get replaced with proof-backed language
- amenities, location context, family relevance, wellness relevance, business relevance, and local experience details become easier for AI systems to retrieve
- the hotel's own narrative has a better chance of showing up before OTA summaries flatten it

The practical point is simple: Tercier produces content that is easier for both a human guest and an AI answer engine to understand, trust, and reuse.

## How the platform is used

The hotel team uses Tercier to:
- inspect which guest segments and demand moments matter now
- compare the property against its comp set by segment or traveler intent
- see where the direct channel and AI-facing narrative are weak
- review the ranked execution queue
- generate or adapt pages, campaign content, and answer assets
- ship work faster without waiting on a long agency cycle

The product experience should feel like an always-on local commercial function inside the hotel group, not a static dashboard.

## What the hotel gets

Tercier produces work every month.

The hotel gets:
- a live view of the property's market, guest, comp-set, and discovery context
- a ranked set of commercial priorities
- recommended page and campaign actions tied to those priorities
- ready-to-ship content drafts and answer assets
- multilingual variations where needed
- a stronger direct-channel and AI-discovery narrative without adding local headcount

The recurring outcome is a team that knows what to push next and already has the assets to act on it.

## Why a hotel buys

A premium hotel buys Tercier because Tercier compresses work that otherwise sits across multiple people, tools, and delays.

Tercier gives the property:
- better local commercial judgment
- faster execution
- better direct-channel messaging
- better AI-discovery readiness
- less reliance on generic HQ support or slow agency cycles

The product earns its place when the team can say:
- the property knows which guests matter this month
- the property knows what messages are weak
- the property has the pages and campaign assets to fix that now

## What Tercier is becoming over time

The first wedge is direct-demand execution.

The broader platform can expand into:
- deeper channel decisioning
- richer AI-discovery monitoring across platforms
- more direct integration into publishing and workflow systems
- stronger entity and brand-control tooling across groups and portfolios
- future agent-readiness layers as travel discovery becomes more transactional inside AI systems
